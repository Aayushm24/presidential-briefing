## Content

> Last week, my manager asked me:
>
> Aayush, are you making "progress", or just creating the "illusion" of it?
>
> I'd spent the last 5 years being an "execution machine." This question broke me.
>
> See, before Atlan, I was the guy who:
> - Shipped things fast
> - Checked off 10+ tasks before lunch
> - Worked mindlessly and felt productive
>
> I wore my “busyness” like a badge of honor.
>
> Then I joined Atlan. Showed my first week's progress.
>
> My manager's response?
>
> "You did A thing”. That's motion, not progress. 
> Actual progress? What metric did it move for us? 
>
> Wait what?
>
> I was confused. Wasn't doing stuff... the job?
>
> Turns out, at Atlan, every project needs:
>
> - A problem/solution statement
> - A clear metric it will move
> - A process diagram
>
> No exceptions.
>
> My first attempt? Painful.
>
> I sat for 3 hours trying to write a P/PS statement. 3 HOURS. 
>
> For what used to take me 3 minutes to just start doing.
>
> Felt like I was wasting time. Doing nothing.
>
> Then something clicked.
>
> Those 3 hours of thinking saved 30 hours of rework.
>
> Now my split looks like (still WIP):
> - 80% thinking
> - 20% doing
>
> The result? I do "slightly" better work in half the time.
>
> My manager put it perfectly:
>
> "Deep thinking takes time & willingness to question. 
> First principles thinking takes time to develop. 
> A powerful habit to incorporate is to make time to think."
>
> Here's what "slowing down" taught me:
>
> The best PMs aren't the fastest typers.
>
> They're the deepest thinkers.
>
> We've been trained to mistake motion for progress. 
> But motion without direction is just expensive wandering.
>
> Sometimes the most productive thing you can do is close your laptop and think.
>
> What percentage of your day is thinking vs doing?
>
> p.s. If your calendar is full of "doing" meetings, you might be stuck in the same trap I was :)
