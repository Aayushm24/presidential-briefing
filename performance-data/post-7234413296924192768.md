> 53 miles 🚄
>
> The distance I traveled today to meet 3 founders...
>
> As we're in our 0-1 journey, we're doing all things that don't scale
>
> (Paul Graham, I hope you're proud of me 🥹) 
>
> The day for Sandeep Talukdar and me was pretty long
>
> It involved a lot of walking, trains, buses, and 1 Lyft ride
>
> We were out of our house by 8:45 am to catch a Caltrain to San Jose
>
> 2 buses later, we met Girish Redekar at 11 am
>
> We discussed:
> - Diff between demand harvesting (market has existing demand) and generation (market needs education)
> - Better to link products to existing problems that people can relate to
> - Use a multi-channel GTM approach and double down on what works
> - Demand generation is 10X harder than  harvesting
>
> At 1 pm, we met Sachin Gupta, we discussed:
> - Target niche segments or specific integrations where there’s less competition, especially in AI space
> - Focus on being the best at a particular function within a smaller market vs competing in a crowded space
> - Find smaller integration partners and become the best at serving their customers
> - Outbound works only when there is good marketing cover
>
> Quick pit stop at Starbucks to grab a bite and then walked to another bus stop 
>
> The last meeting of the day was with Bo LEI at 3:00 Pm
>
> We discussed:
> - Focus on building a single, dense use case that addresses a specific problem
> - Avoid spreading too thin by trying to solve too many problems at once
> - Leverage niche webinars or events as an effective early-stage GTM strategy
>
> He was kind enough to drop us to a nearby train station which took us back to the San Jose train station.
>
> 1 Cal train and some walking later we reached home at 6:30 PM and were dead tired.
>
> p.s. On the ride back home, I continued gathering my thoughts about why knowledge management sucks today 😅
