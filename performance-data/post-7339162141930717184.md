# My mom asked AI to cancel her dentist appointment.

## Content

> My mom asked AI to cancel her dentist appointment.
>
> It wrote a LinkedIn message.
>
> 147 words.
> 3 paragraphs.
> "I am writing to formally request..."
>
> She deleted it all.
>
> Typed: "Can't make Saturday"
>
> Sent.
>
> That's when it hit me.
>
> Every AI tool today is broken the same way:
>
> - Gmail AI turns 5 words into 50
> - Notion AI explains your notes back to you
> - Calendar AI suggests meetings nobody wants
> - Grammarly makes everyone sound like robots
>
> We put rocket engines on bicycles.
> Then wonder why we keep falling over.
>
> Here's the thing:
>
> Instead of: "AI, help me write emails"
> Should be: "AI, handle all my emails"
>
> Instead of: "AI, summarize this doc"
> Should be: "AI, just show me what matters"
>
> Instead of: "AI, improve my writing"
> Should be: "AI, write like me so I don't have to"
>
> See the difference?
>
> Current AI = Faster way to do boring work
> Real AI = Never do boring work again
>
> Mom doesn't want a smarter assistant.
>
> She wants her appointment canceled.
> In 5 words.
> Like she asked.
>
> AI should do what we want, how we want it, without us having to manage it.
>
> All she wanted was a simple email. AI made it complex.
>
> But we'll get there.
> We always do.
>
> BTW - Mom asked if she can train Gmail by yelling at it like Siri. I said no. She said "Then what's the point?"
>
> Mom: 2 | AI: 0
>
> p.s. inspired by Pete Koomen's "AI Horseless Carriages" - a must-read on why we're building AI wrong
