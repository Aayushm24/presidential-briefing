## Content

> every startup says they're "ai-powered" now.
>
> but 99% are just chatgpt wrappers with extra steps.
>
> the 1% though? they're terrifying.
>
> Don't get me WRONG, the 99% aren't dumb. 
> Smart companies. Great founders. Wrong approach.
>
> Here's why:
>
> They're still thinking AI First when winners are going AI Native.
>
> Most companies don't even know there's a difference.
>
> There is. And it changes everything.
>
> See, there are two types of companies right now:
>
> AI First: "How can we add AI to improve X?" 
> AI Native: "Why does X exist at all?"
>
> One optimizes. One eliminates.
>
> At Atlan, we realized going AI Native means transforming at 3 levels:
>
> - AI Native Departments: Reimagining what Marketing, Sales, Product even do
> - AI Native Talent: Every single person thinking with AI, not just using it
> - AI Native Operations: Breaking down silos, AI-powered collaboration
>
> Khosla Ventures (the $15 Bn VC) gets us:
> They're buying call centers and rebuilding them AI Native.
> Not improving them. Replacing their entire existence.
>
> The uncomfortable truth:
> If you're adding AI features, you're playing defense. 
> If you're eliminating human constraints, you're playing offense.
>
> Most of your processes exist because humans have limitations:
> - Can't work 24/7
> - Can't predict what they don't know
> - Can't process 1000 data points simultaneously
>
> Kill the constraint. Kill the process.
>
> Ask yourself: What would you never have built if AI always existed?
>
> Your answer might be your biggest opportunity.
>
> Or your biggest threat if someone else figures it out first.
>
> What would you delete if AI had always existed?
>
> p.s. My honest answer? My entire role. That's why I'm learning to build what replaces me. 
> p.p.s This is me drooling over our new N8N setup 👇🏻
