## Content

> Last week I discovered I'm terrible
> at the one thing I pride myself on:
>
> Clear communication.
>
> The evidence was overwhelming.
>
> I literally get paid to make 
> complex AI workflows simple.
>
> Then I wrote a "simple" team announcement.
>
> Enter Suren.
>
> "Nice announcement. But how many
> things did you assume people know?"
>
> I thought "Maybe 5-6 things?"
>
> "Let's check."
>
> We asked AI to count.
> Every. Single. Assumption.
>
> 36.
>
> Thirty-six assumptions. In one message.
>
> Like believing people:
> - Know what "n8n" means
> - Will brainstorm great use cases
> - See why AI enablement matters
> - Understand what are AI workflows
> (and more.....)
>
> My "clear" message?
> Cryptic.
>
> Written in a language
> only I understand.
>
> Here's what hit me:
>
> We're all doing this.
> Every email. Every presentation.
> Every "simple" explanation.
>
> The curse of knowledge is real.
>
> The more you know,
> the worse you communicate.
>
> You literally forget
> what it's like to "not" know.
>
> So here's my question:
>
> What obvious thing do you say daily that's probably not obvious at all?
