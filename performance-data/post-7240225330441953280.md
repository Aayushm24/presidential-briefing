## Content

> 2 weeks back, I met an old lady in Berkley...
>
> She was lost and had been walking for 50 minutes to figure out where she lived
>
> She was distraught, tired, and 100% puzzled
>
> I met her while I was crossing the road to buy some ice cream
>
> I started talking to her to figure out where she stayed and tried my best to help
>
> However, she couldn't trust me, and whenever I probed further she would ask me to leave
>
> She kept repeating "This is my neighborhood, I have lived here for 22 years"
>
> I tried multiple times to help her but to no avail
>
> She just asked how she could reach Hearst and I gave her directions
>
> Cut to 30 minutes later, after I'm done with my ice cream shopping
>
> I took the same route to see if she made it, but as fate would have it, she unfortunately did not :(
>
> No doubt she was lost
>
> So for the next 45 minutes, I tried convincing her to trust me
>
> I politely asked her to check her pockets and see if she had a phone, a card, or maybe a number to call
>
> After tons of pleading and requesting, she emptied her pockets:
> - I found a Berkley PD card
> - An illegible receipt
> - Keys
>
> The saving grace? The Berkley PD card had a number and an address written on it.
>
> We googled to see how far the address was and it said 2 miles.
>
> She couldn't believe it.
>
> I tried showing her photos of the address and she said well yeah this looks slightly familiar.
>
> To me, it felt like she had early signs of dementia, so the best course of action would be to call Berkley PD
>
> We finally did, and the police showed up in the next 10 minutes.
>
> Interestingly, they already knew the context as the same thing happened 1 day before.
>
> So happy everything turned out fine 🙏🏻
>
> It's weird how the world works sometimes
>
> p.s. This is not a Samaritan post, but a reflection on how the tech world is moving so fast, yet nobody is solving this.
>
> p.p.s I'm not medically diagnosing her, that was my first reaction in the moment
