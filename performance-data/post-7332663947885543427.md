## Content

> I quit my job with zero backup plan.
>
> Everyone said I was insane.
>
> 3 months later, I realized they were wrong about everything.
>
> See, by January 2025, I was done:
> - Mentally fried
> - Living on autopilot
> - Couldn't give 100% at Springworks
>
> So I did what "responsible adults" never do.
>
> I walked away.
>
> - No next job lined up. 
> - No 12-month runway saved.
> - No side hustle to fall back on. 
>
> Just... nothing.
>
> My savings dropped 𝟰𝟬%. But, my anxiety dropped 𝟵𝟬%.
>
> For the first time in my life, my calendar was empty.
>
> And something strange happened. I became the happiest I'd ever been.
>
> - Random coffee at 2pm? Sure. 
> - Reading for 6 hours straight? Why not. 
> - Deep diving into AI with no agenda? Absolutely.
>
> Then I did something even crazier.
>
> A, 10-day Vipassana retreat:
> - No phone
> - No talking
> - 4:30am wake-ups
> - 10 hours of meditation daily
>
> The hardest thing I've ever done. Also the most transformative.
>
> Here's what 3 months of "nothing" taught me:
>
> The space between your last thing and your next thing?
>
> That's not emptiness. 
> That's where you find yourself again.
>
> We're so obsessed with the next milestone that we forget:
>
> Sometimes not knowing 𝗜𝗦 the plan.
>
> If you can "afford" to take a break, do it. 
>
> Not a vacation. Not a sabbatical with goals.
>
> A real break. Where nothing is planned and everything is possible.
>
> It might just be the most productive thing you never do.
>
> p.s. Take this post as your sign to take a break from work :)
>
> p.p.s First day at the Vipassana center 👇🏻
