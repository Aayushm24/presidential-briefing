## Content

> I asked AI one simple question about our future...
>
> The answer was terrifying
>
> My prompt was simple:
>
> "Based on how we operate as humans today, create an image showing humanity in 50 years if we continue this behavior"
>
> AI had infinite futures to choose from.
>
> Medical miracles. Space colonies. Green cities. Mundane progress. Radical transformation.
>
> It chose dystopia.
>
> Respirator masks. Scorched skies. Dead rivers. Digital billboards selling fake happiness.
>
> Every sci-fi nightmare, perfectly rendered.
>
> But here's what's surprising:
>
> I never asked for dystopia.
>
> AI just assumed that's what I meant.
>
> Because that's what we've taught it "the future" means.
>
> Through every:
> - Climate headline we share
> - Apocalypse movie we stream
> - Doomsday prediction we click
> - Black Mirror episode we binge
>
> We've trained AI that future equals fear.
>
> The most advanced prediction engine ever built can only imagine one tomorrow:
>
> The one where everything breaks.
> Not because that's inevitable.
> Because that's all we feed it.
>
> What does it say about us
> when our machines can't dream
> of anything but nightmares?
