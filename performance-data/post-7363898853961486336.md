## Content

> Your AI startup isn't a SaaS company.
>
> It’s an all-you-can-eat buffet where your customers get twice as hungry every 6 months.
>
> The $20/month flat-fee pricing model is a trap.
>
> Founders are betting the farm on a simple assumption:
> As compute gets cheaper, our margins will grow.
>
> This is a fatal oversimplification.
>
> It ignores the other side of the equation:
> As AI gets more powerful, our appetite for it explodes.
>
> Last year, a user was happy summarizing a 1-page doc.
>
> Today, they want to analyze a 200-page PDF and ask 50 follow-up questions.
>
> Your costs for that user just went up 100x.
> But the cost of compute didn't drop 100x.
> And even if it did, our ambition would just expand 1000x to meet it.
>
> You're left holding the bag.
>
> So what's the playbook? 
>
> In his piece on this, Ethan Ding suggests three paths to survive:
>
> 1. Usage-Based Pricing: 
> Stop selling a buffet. Charge for every plate.
>
> 2. High Switching Costs: 
> Integrate deeply into a customer's workflow (especially enterprise) that leaving becomes too painful.
>
> 3. Vertical Integration: 
> Make the AI a loss-leader for other, highly profitable services like hosting or databases.
>
> The common thread is you must build a business model that profits from explosive use, instead of being drowned by it.
>
> Which path are you choosing?
