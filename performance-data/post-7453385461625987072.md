## Content

> OpenAI released GPT-5.5.
>
> And my feed is already doing the thing it always does.
>
> Spreadsheets comparing price per million tokens.
>
> Threads weighing whether the capability bump justifies the cost.
>
> Teams publicly agonizing over this upgrade.
>
> Then Lenny Rachitsky tested it.
>
> He ran Codex scenarios where GPT-4o consistently failed. 
>
> GPT-5.5 handled them: complete code generation, accurate multi-step reasoning, the works.
>
> His reaction ↓
> "Happy to pay $180 per million tokens."
>
> That's a tell.
>
> When a model crosses into capabilities the old one couldn't touch, you don't 
> debate the price. You pay it and move on.
>
> The debate only exists when the gains are marginal.
>
> When a jump like this lands, the advantage almost always outweighs the cost
>
> My take is teams with room in their AI budget just unlocked a new capability. Teams without just got locked out of it.
>
> The company that can afford frontier inference can now run support, research, and operations that competitors on tighter budgets genuinely cannot match.
>
> For two years, the story was that AI democratized capability. Anyone with a decent prompt could compete.
>
> That story is reversing FAST.
>
> Frontier capability is starting to cost frontier money. And capability is becoming 
> a function of budget, not just talent.
>
> Did you try GPT 5.5 yet? I haven't but I'm excited...
