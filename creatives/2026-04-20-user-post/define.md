# Define — 2026-04-20 user post (12 months)

## Source post

```
AI startups have 12 months before they die…
Every week I watch a category shrink.
The pattern is always the same.
Small team ships a clever AI wrapper.
Founders raise a round.
Six months later, Anthropic or OpenAI ships the same thing natively.
The startup pivots or dies quietly.
Most people I talk to are building the exact thing OpenAI will ship next quarter. They just don't see it yet.
The ones who survive are building moats the platforms can't copy.
There are only three that still hold:

- Distribution
- Proprietary data
- Deep workflow integration

Canva has 100 million users. When they decide your AI feature belongs in their workflow, you're not competing against their product. You're competing against their audience.
That's distribution.
Customer support logs from 2023 aren't a moat. Real-time workflow data from power users who can't leave is.
That's proprietary data.
And deep integration isn't "we added AI to our app." It's going so far into the job that switching means rebuilding the whole workflow from scratch.
That's the third.
Everything else is a feature waiting to be absorbed.
Notion rebuilt their agent system five times before shipping.
Every time they got close, a platform provider shipped the feature underneath them.
They only won by going deeper, not wider.
I doubt current stakes are about model access anymore. That war is over.
IMO, it's about who owns the user when the feature becomes commodity.
Small teams can still win.
But "build the feature, they'll come" is a 2023 strategy running on 2026 timelines.
what are you building that only YOU can build?
```

## Position statement

Make an AI founder feel that their current roadmap has a 12-month expiration date, and forward this post to their co-founder before closing LinkedIn.

## 3 conviction bets (specific, falsifiable)

1. **Alarm-not-chart** — Posting a 240px red "12" with the word MONTHS below will out-stop a neat three-column pillar chart, because the post's hook is a deadline, not a framework. Infographics about survival read colder than a countdown does.
2. **His-words-not-ours** — Typesetting his actual sentence ("AI startups have 12 months before they die…") huge, ellipsis included, beats paraphrasing into tight agency copy. The hesitation in the ellipsis is the voice.
3. **Question-wins-closer** — The closing question ("what are you building that only YOU can build?") works harder as a standalone negative-space card than as a footer on a list card. The reader's inner monologue finishes the creative.

## Kill list (≥5 explicit "will NOT do" items)

1. Will NOT use gradients on backgrounds (reads as 2015 startup deck).
2. Will NOT use stock illustrations, grim-reaper clip art, skull icons, or tombstones (the post is serious, not meme-bait).
3. Will NOT use more than one hero color per creative (Coral hero only; amber accent max 5% area).
4. Will NOT center-align any hero element (F-pattern scan requires top-left anchor).
5. Will NOT use MBA vocabulary not in his post ("commoditization", "application layer", "differentiation" — banned). "moats" IS allowed because Aayush uses it 4 times in the post.
6. Will NOT add "presidential briefing" anywhere. Footer is LinkedIn icon + aayush-maheshwari + 2026-04-20 only.
7. Will NOT include Aayush's photo or avatar. The claim stands on its own.
8. Will NOT use emoji as design elements.
9. Will NOT round corners over 32px (toy-like).
10. Will NOT write any text under 14px.

## Success criteria

- **3-second test:** The viewer leaves with two facts in under 3 seconds — "AI startups have 12 months" and "three things save you: distribution, data, deep integration" (or, for mechanic-specific cards, the single big idea of that card). If they can't say one of those without re-scanning, the option fails.
- **Thumbnail test:** At 400px wide, the hero (the number, the question, or the contrast) must still read. Body copy can melt; the hero cannot.
- **Logo-swap test:** If I replaced `aayush-maheshwari` with a random handle, the creative should still feel like a founder wrote it, not an agency. Specifically: his lowercase "what", his ellipsis, his word "moats", the IMO tone. Swap those for MBA copy and the voice dies — that's the proof the voice is load-bearing.

## Design constraints

- **Mood:** Coral urgency (`--hero: #dc2626` red-600, `--accent: #f59e0b` amber-500, `--surface: #fafaf9`, `--card: #ffffff`, `--ink: #0a0a0a`, `--ink-dim: #52525b`, `--border: #e4e4e7`). Picked because this post is the "wake up" archetype — a deadline post about quiet death. Violet thesis is too philosophical for a 12-month timer. Coral is earned here.
- **Typography:** Inter only. Hero 140–260px / weight 800–900 / tracking -0.03 to -0.05em. Headline 48–72px / 700–800. Body 18–22px / 400–500. Chip 12–14px / 600 / UPPERCASE +0.05em.
- **Mandatory elements:** Use Aayush's actual words (tight excerpts, no paraphrase). Footer = LinkedIn SVG icon + `aayush-maheshwari` + `2026-04-20`. 1200×1200 square.
- **Forbidden elements:** Anything in the kill list above. No "presidential briefing". No multi-accent colors. No gradients. No stock art.
- **Dimensions:** 1200×1200 for all 5 (LinkedIn feed sweet spot).
- **Color allocation:** 60% surface / 30% card+secondary / 10% hero+accent combined. Amber accent used once per creative, max.
