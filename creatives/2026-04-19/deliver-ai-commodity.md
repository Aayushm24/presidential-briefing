## Per-option critique

### Option 1: The Commodity — **REVISE**
- Hero sentence carries most weight, but "commodity." (slate) competes with "AI isn't your competitive advantage" (black) at similar scale. The word that IS the thesis doesn't get enough dominance.
- **Conviction bet #1 (commodity as hero word): PARTIALLY VALIDATED.** It's there but shares stage.
- Fix: cut the setup clause from prelude to 1 line, drop hero claim to 2 lines max, boost "commodity." to 20% larger than surrounding words.
- 3-second test: PASS (gets the thesis). Thumbnail: PASS. Logo-swap: PASS (Aayush-specific framing).

### Option 2: Same Week — **SHIP (context-specific)**
- Strong when the audience is skeptical and wants evidence. Two data points + slate-block thesis at bottom closes the argument.
- **Kill list check:** no brand logos, no gradients, single accent ✓
- Weakness: busier than the other options → less scroll-stop, more "stop-and-read-if-already-interested."
- Use when: repost to an existing audience, or when pairing with a long-form caption.

### Option 3: The Stack — **SHIP (structural thesis)**
- Best at TEACHING the thesis. Fading layers above, slate moat-block below. The visual IS the argument.
- Hierarchy is textbook: eye lands on title → scans faded layers → lands hard on slate block. F-pattern with a terminal anchor.
- **Conviction bet #2 (slate for philosophical weight): VALIDATED.**
- Use when: thought-leadership angle, decks, or pinned post.

### Option 4: One Word — **SHIP (THE WINNER)**
- Scroll-stop rating 10/10. 70% negative space + `commodity.` in 186px slate.
- **Conviction bet #3 (negative space > supporting text): VALIDATED.** The emptiness forces absorption of the thesis.
- 3-second test: PASS (one word does the job). Thumbnail test: PASS at 400px. Logo-swap test: PASS — generic version would be "AI is overhyped"; this is specific to Aayush's layer-thinking conviction.
- Kill list: no gradients, no emoji, no stock, single slate accent, top-left anchor ✓
- Weakness: relies on caption carrying the proof. The image alone is pure thesis.

### Option 5: Find Your Texas — **KILL (this round)**
- Wrong line to hero. "Smart constraint beats perfect general solution" is tactical advice, not the conviction of this brief.
- Would work for a FOLLOW-UP post about constraint. Save the structure, swap the content.

## Verdict

**Ship Option 4 as lead creative.** Use Option 3 as reinforcement if a second slot is needed (e.g., repost the same post with the other visual two days later). Option 2 works for a data-skeptic audience segment — keep as backup.

## What Option 4 proves

Conviction bet #3 was the highest-risk bet — "negative space wins against supporting text." It would have been tempting to fill the canvas with proof points. The critique confirms: filling would have neutered the thesis. The empty space IS the conviction.
