# Define — ai-commodity (Option 1)

## Source post

> AI isn't your competitive advantage. It's a commodity.
>
> Cerebras filed for IPO with a $10+ billion OpenAI deal. Tesla scaled robotaxis to three Texas cities, all generating real revenue.
>
> Same week.
>
> When specialized chips go public and autonomous systems work at commercial scale, the moat isn't the model anymore. It's not the compute. It's what you build on top.
>
> The teams winning right now pick constrained contexts where AI works today. Tesla didn't launch everywhere — they picked Texas. Smart constraint beats perfect general solution.
>
> Most AI products fail because they skip memory, not because of the model. Context that persists. State that learns. Systems that remember.
>
> While everyone obsesses over model upgrades, the real advantage lives in the application layer.
>
> what constrained AI use case are you proving works?

## Position statement

Make a builder feel that their current "AI-first" pitch is exposing them as a follower, and want to forward this to the founder they're advising so that founder re-examines where their moat actually is.

## 3 conviction bets (falsifiable)

1. **"Commodity" as the hero word will land harder than "$10B"**
   Because stats are what people expect on LinkedIn (chartjunk territory). A single framing word that reframes the whole category is rarer and stops the thumb because the reader has to confirm what they just read. Testable: A/B a commodity-hero card vs a $10B-hero card.

2. **Deep slate (near-black) accent will beat indigo/violet for this specific claim**
   Because the post is philosophical, not celebratory. Deep slate reads as "final word" / "thesis"; indigo reads as tech-company-pitch. Testable: if indigo made this post feel like a VC slide, slate should feel like a manifesto.

3. **Negative space below the claim is worth more than any supporting text**
   Because the claim is a full sentence on its own and the reader doesn't need proof at glance level — they need time to absorb. 60-70% empty canvas forces the eye back to the claim instead of scattering. Testable: add supporting text and the hook weakens.

## Kill list

1. Will NOT use gradients — reads as 2015 startup deck.
2. Will NOT use NVIDIA / Cerebras / Tesla logos — turns the creative into a news graphic, kills the philosophical weight.
3. Will NOT use the $10B number as the hero — it's a supporting fact, not the claim. Using it as hero drops this into generic AI-news-card territory.
4. Will NOT include a photo of Aayush — the claim should stand without the person; adding a face shrinks the thesis.
5. Will NOT use emoji in the hero — emoji decoration reads as Twitter thread, not POV.
6. Will NOT center-align the hero text — F-pattern scan requires top-left anchor.
7. Will NOT add a CTA button — the post's last line IS the CTA, and the creative carries the claim not the action.

## Success criteria

- **3-second test:** viewer gets "AI is a commodity" + "commodity chips + autonomous driving are why" + "the moat is elsewhere" in three seconds.
- **Thumbnail test:** at 400px wide, the word "commodity" must still be legible and bold enough to carry the message alone.
- **Logo-swap test:** if `@aayush-maheshwari` were replaced with `@randomfounder`, the post should feel off — because "commodity" is Aayush's conviction-stack (he pairs it with memory/application-layer thinking), not a general AI take. Generic version would read as "AI is overhyped" and lose the specificity.

## Design constraints

- **Color palette:** ONE accent — **slate (#0f172a)**. Philosophical weight, thesis tone, not pitch-deck energy. Background stays `#f8f9fb` (off-white).
- **Typography:** Inter only. Hero 140-180px weight 900, supporting 22-28px weight 500.
- **Mandatory elements:** the word "commodity" (hero), the setup clause, the week's two proof points (Cerebras IPO + Tesla robotaxis), @aayush-maheshwari handle, date chip.
- **Forbidden elements:** see kill list. Also: no hashtags, no brand logos, no stock iconography, no big numbers in the hero position.
- **Dimensions:** 1200×1200 (LinkedIn square — best for feed scan + high real-estate).
