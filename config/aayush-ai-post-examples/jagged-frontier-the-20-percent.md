# Example: Jagged frontier — the 20% AI can't touch

## Context

- **Date written:** 2026-04-27
- **Theme:** AI automation skeptics, the jagged frontier of capability, augmentation > automation
- **Trigger:** The flood of "AI automation expert" pitches in Aayush's LinkedIn feed
- **Why this matters as an anchor:** Second confirmed Register B post (after GPT-5.5 "my feed is doing the thing" on 2026-04-24). Validates the two-register framework. Also the second corpus instance of the **two-line binary hook** — graduates that pattern from "variant of A" to confirmed.

## Awareness level

**L2 — Aware-unsure.** Reader knows AI automation is being sold everywhere; they're unsure how to evaluate what's real vs hype. The post reframes the evaluation criterion at a deeper level: stop asking what AI can do, start asking where it should stop.

## Post (verbatim, preserve line breaks)

```
Everybody these days is an AI automation expert.

It's a great service to sell to founders.

A founder will see AI excel in one domain. Then assume it works the same way everywhere else.

They pitch "80% automation" without understanding the remaining 20% might be the most critical part.

The pattern shows up everywhere:

- Marketing thinks sales is "just sending emails."
- Sales ops thinks accounting is "just entering numbers."
- Product thinks customer service is "just answering questions."

All three are wrong about what AI can replace.

The jagged frontier is real.

AI writes perfect code for complex algorithms but fails at counting characters in a string.

It handles creative tasks that seemed impossible two years ago and breaks on basic reasoning any human solves instantly.

Teams build for the peaks. Crash on the valleys.

When building with AI, the question that matters more than "what can AI do" is "where should it stop."

The successful automation tools I track all have one thing in common.

They don't promise full automation.

They design human handoff points from day one.

AI handles what it's good at. Humans handle the rest. The interface between them IS the product.

That's augmentation, not automation.

The experts selling 80% automation are skipping the part that decides whether their tool ships or fails.

What's the 20% in your stack that AI absolutely cannot touch?
```

## Structural devices worth naming

1. **Two-line binary hook** — `Everybody these days is an AI automation expert.` / (line break) / `It's a great service to sell to founders.` Two-sentence punch where line 1 is a flat observation and line 2 is the cynical/sharp reframe. **Corpus: 2/5 finalized posts now** (this + Claude Code "Everyone has it. Almost no one is using it right.") — graduates from "variant of Pattern A" to confirmed.

2. **Dismissive-quote bullet list (NEW, single-sighting)** — `[Role A] thinks [domain B] is "just [trivializing verb phrase]"`. Three parallel bullets where each bullet is one team's flat dismissal of another team's actual complexity. Format: `- Marketing thinks sales is "just sending emails."` Use when criticizing how teams underestimate adjacent domains. **Corpus: 0/5 (this is the first sighting). Annotate, do not promote yet.**

3. **"All [N] are wrong" recap after a numbered list** — drops in immediately after the bullet list as a one-line tag. `All three are wrong about what AI can replace.` Functions like a "That's distribution." recap but anchored to a count. Pair this with hyphen-bullet 3-item lists.

4. **Concept-naming tag** — `That's augmentation, not automation.` 4-word punch after the meaning is built up. Variant of the "That's distribution." pattern (4 corpus instances + this).

5. **Parallel-imperative rhythm** — `Teams build for the peaks. Crash on the valleys.` Two short imperatives, same subject elided in the second. Already a confirmed device.

6. **Meta-skill reframe** — `When building with AI, the question that matters more than "what can AI do" is "where should it stop."` The "the question is not X, the question is Y" framing names the meta-skill the reader should adopt. Corpus has variations (Post 1 "What's the ugliest workaround in your current agent setup?", Post 2 "who's actually going to be using this thing?") but the explicit "matters more than X is Y" form is novel-ish — annotate.

7. **ALL CAPS one-word emphasis (NEW, single-sighting)** — `The interface between them IS the product.` Caps applied to a small linking word for stress. **Corpus: 0/5 (grep didn't match `\bIS\b` as standalone). Annotate, do not promote yet.**

8. **Closing question — specific present-tense self-check** — `What's the 20% in your stack that AI absolutely cannot touch?` The closer maps the abstract claim to the reader's own situation, with a concrete handle ("your stack", "the 20%"). 5/5 corpus posts close with a specific question. Confirmed.

## Things this post does NOT do (anti-patterns to avoid)

- **No At Atlan grounding beat** — confirms Register B (observer-mode without operator-stake)
- **No p.s. / p.p.s. closer** — the closing question IS the close
- **No unicode-bold numbers** (no pivot number; "80% / 20%" is the reference anchor, not the punchline)
- **No emojis** anywhere
- **No personal first-person experience** ("I see this play out with most GTM teams" is observer voice, not biographical)
- **No hashtags**
- **No second thesis** — the original draft had a regulatory-timing thesis tacked onto the jagged-frontier core; the finalized version cuts it. **One Story Per Post rule held.**

## When to copy this post's structure

- The story is about a category of pitches/products that share the same flaw
- The flaw is a faulty belief that AI is uniformly capable across domains
- The reader is an operator or builder evaluating tools/services (L2 awareness — knows the problem exists)
- You can name 3 concrete examples of the dismissive belief in the form `[Role] thinks [adjacent domain] is "just [trivializing thing]"`
- A meta-skill or reframed-question can land the implication

Do NOT copy when the story is:
- A frontier capability launch (use `gpt-55-my-feed-is-doing-the-thing` instead)
- An Aayush personal-experience piece (no precedent yet — would need a TIER 1 anchor)
- A specific product or company change (use `ai-startups-12-months` or `every-saas-ai-button` for category critique with named examples)
