# Example: GPT-5.5 "my feed is doing the thing"

## Context

- **Date written:** 2026-04-24
- **Theme:** AI model pricing, frontier access gap, democratization reversal
- **Trigger:** OpenAI launched GPT-5.5 at $180/M output tokens; Lenny Rachitsky tested it in Codex scenarios
- **Why this matters as an anchor:** Aayush wrote this himself after reading the pipeline's Option 2. His version is noticeably more his voice than what we shipped. Treat it as voice-truth for the "AI model / pricing / capability" beat.

## Post (verbatim, preserve line breaks)

```
OpenAI released a new model.



GPT-5.5.



And my feed is already doing the thing it always does.



Spreadsheets comparing price per million tokens.



Threads weighing whether the capability bump justifies the cost.



Teams publicly agonizing over this upgrade.



Then Lenny Rachitsky tested it.



He ran Codex scenarios where GPT-4o consistently failed.



GPT-5.5 handled them: complete code generation, accurate multi-step reasoning, the works.



His reaction ↓

"Happy to pay $180 per million tokens."



That's a tell.



When a model crosses into capabilities the old one couldn't touch, you don't
debate the price. You pay it and move on.



The debate only exists when the gains are marginal.



When a jump like this lands, the advantage almost always outweighs the cost



My take is teams with room in their AI budget just unlocked a new capability. Teams without just got locked out of it.



The company that can afford frontier inference can now run support, research, and operations that competitors on tighter budgets genuinely cannot match.



For two years, the story was that AI democratized capability. Anyone with a decent prompt could compete.



That story is reversing FAST.



Frontier capability is starting to cost frontier money. And capability is becoming
a function of budget, not just talent.



Did you try GPT 5.5 yet? I haven't my I'm excited...
```

## Structural devices worth naming

1. **Observation-first opening (no hook-formula).** The first 6 paragraphs are literally "here is what I'm watching happen." No contrarian reveal, no personal story, no data-point. Just watching the feed. The reader gets pulled in because Aayush is pointing at something they're also seeing.

2. **Fragment paragraphs, aggressively.** Every paragraph is 1 or 2 sentences. Double (sometimes triple) blank lines between them. The whitespace is the rhythm. Do not combine these into flowing prose.

3. **Named-source pivot.** "Then Lenny Rachitsky tested it." This is the turn — the post stops describing the noise and starts describing a specific person's specific test. Named entity, specific action. Always a named source when pivoting.

4. **Explicit ↓ arrow + pull-quote.** `His reaction ↓\n"Happy to pay $180 per million tokens."` — the arrow makes the quote land. Use this when the punchline belongs to someone you're quoting, not something you're asserting.

5. **"That's a tell." three-word reset.** One sentence, one beat. Works after a quote or named action to lock in the interpretation. Pattern: `That's [N].` or `[Name] is [doing N].` — then a new paragraph unpacks why.

6. **"My take is ..." marker.** When the post pivots from observation to opinion, he explicitly labels it. Honest, not hedged, not decorated. Do not use "IMO" and "My take is" in the same post — pick one.

7. **ALL CAPS single word.** `That story is reversing FAST.` One word, not a phrase. Rare enough to still hit. Do not overuse — one ALL CAPS word per post max.

8. **Unresolved closer.** `Did you try GPT 5.5 yet? I haven't my I'm excited...` — a question the writer hasn't answered for himself, with a typo-like run-on ("I haven't my I'm") that keeps the rhythm of thought. The post ends in uncertainty, not resolution. That is load-bearing — it signals "this is me thinking, not me teaching."

## Things this post does NOT do (anti-patterns to avoid)

- **No `p.s.` closer.** When the natural closer is a genuine question, no p.s. or p.p.s. This is a posting mode, not a rule — other posts do use p.s. effectively.
- **No unicode-bold numbers** (like 𝟯𝟲). Plain text only.
- **No `**bold**` hashtag-style inline emphasis.** Emphasis comes from ALL CAPS on one word and from fragment paragraphs, not from markdown.
- **No emojis.** Zero. Even the otherwise-acceptable 🤷🏻‍♂️ or 👇🏻 don't appear.
- **No Atlan mention.** Not every post needs "I build AI agents at Atlan" or "At Atlan we've been..." — this post has no personal-company hook and works anyway.
- **No hashtag list at the bottom.**
- **No "p.s. at Atlan we've been building X, happy to compare notes."** Outside the registration this post is operating in.

## When to copy this post's structure

Use as the structural template when the story is:
- A frontier model / product launch where someone specific has tested it
- A pricing or access shift that the feed is already reacting to
- A reversal of a prior narrative ("democratization" → "stratification")

Do NOT copy when the story is:
- An Aayush personal-experience piece (use `quit-springworks` or similar)
- A product-mechanism explanation (needs denser prose)
- A team/org pattern where specific company examples carry the post
