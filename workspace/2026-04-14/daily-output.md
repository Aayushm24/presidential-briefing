# Daily Brief — 2026-04-14

## Lead Story
**Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch signals IPO readiness as AI agents fuel revenue surge**

## Briefing
Vercel just told the world it is ready to go public, and the reason it gave should change how you think about who wins in the AI era. At the HumanX conference in San Francisco last week, CEO Guillermo Rauch laid out a thesis that is simple but has enormous implications: the number of people who can build and ship software has exploded, and every single one of those new creators needs somewhere to host what they make. Vercel, the 10-year-old developer tools and hosting platform, is positioning itself as that somewhere. The numbers back up the confidence. Vercel's annual recurring revenue has grown from $100 million at the start of 2024 to a $340 million run rate by the end of February 2026, according to Forbes. That is 3.4x growth in roughly two years for a company that was already at scale. The engine behind it is not a pivot to AI. It is the downstream effect of AI making app creation trivially easy for millions of people who never wrote a line of code before.

This matters because it settles a debate that has been running since ChatGPT launched. Plenty of people argued that AI would commoditize software and crush the companies that serve developers. What is actually happening is the opposite, at least for infrastructure. When Cursor, Bolt, Lovable, Replit, and a dozen other AI coding tools help a non-developer spin up a working app in an afternoon, that app still needs to be deployed, served, scaled, and monitored. Vercel, along with competitors like Netlify, Cloudflare Pages, and AWS Amplify, sits at exactly that chokepoint. Rauch put it bluntly: "The total addressable market of infrastructure has now grown, and it simply has no ceiling." He is not wrong. If the number of people capable of creating production software goes from tens of millions to hundreds of millions, the hosting layer does not shrink. It balloons.

The IPO signal itself is notable for its timing. The tech IPO window has been largely frozen. A company signaling IPO readiness in this environment is not wishful thinking. It is confidence backed by a revenue curve that speaks for itself.
