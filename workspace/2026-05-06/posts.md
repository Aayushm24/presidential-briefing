## OPTION 3, contrarian (hook score: 8)

**Conviction:** L2: Voice AI consolidated around business money while founders chase consumer apps.

**Post:**
Everyone's celebrating ElevenLabs' $500M ARR.

I think they're missing the real story.

Voice AI consolidated around business infrastructure while most founders build consumer chat apps.

$500M in recurring revenue doesn't happen by accident. It happens when paying customers have budget line items for your category.

Companies have moved beyond experimenting with voice. They're operationalizing it.

Customer service workflows route through voice APIs by default. Training content gets generated at voice scale. Product experiences ship voice-first, not text-plus-voice.

The consumer story everyone talks about: "users will chat with AI instead of typing."

The enterprise story nobody talks about: "workflows will speak instead of displaying interfaces."

Two completely different futures.

Consumer apps compete on engagement and retention. Infrastructure competes on reliability and cost per transaction.

ElevenLabs chose business infrastructure. $0.02 per minute, real-time processing, API-first architecture.

They built the pipes, not the apps.

Most AI founders I know are building the apps, not the pipes.

Chat interfaces for end users. Creative tools for content creators. Productivity features for knowledge workers.

All great markets. But none of them have $500M ARR yet.

The money is flowing to the companies that make everyone else's AI products possible, not the products themselves.

Infrastructure eats features. Every time.

IMO, there's a lesson here for every AI company: ask whether you're building what customers use, or what other builders need to make their customers successful.

Because the builders pay business prices. The customers expect consumer prices.

Which market are you actually in?
