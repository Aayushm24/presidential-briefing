## OPTION 3, personal-discovery (hook score: 8)

**Conviction:** L3: Forward Deployed Engineering is becoming necessary for enterprise AI deals because enterprise AI implementations are custom by definition and require embedded engineering teams, not traditional SaaS self-serve models.

**Post:**
Amazon just committed $1 billion to embed engineers inside enterprises.

Following OpenAI. Following Anthropic.

A few founders I spoke with this week asked the same question: do we need our own FDE capability?

The answer is probably yes.

When we build agents at Atlan, the model is the easy part. The hard part is understanding each customer's data formats, compliance requirements, existing system integrations, and workflow patterns.

That's not a consulting problem. That's a forward-deployed engineering problem.

Cookie-cutter AI products cannot handle this complexity.

Traditional implementation consulting can't either, because the technology is too new and changes too quickly.

Forward deployed engineers bridge this gap by embedding directly inside customer organizations for months-long implementations. They understand both the AI technology and the customer's business context deeply enough to build custom solutions that actually work in production.

This is expensive but necessary for large enterprise deals.

Sierra's Natalie Meurer describes how the FDE role is converging with product engineering. These are not consultants or field engineers. They are product builders who happen to work inside customer organizations.

They ship code that becomes part of the product.

Amazon's $1 billion investment follows the same pattern. The unit economics work because enterprise AI contracts are large enough to justify embedded engineering costs.

The strategic value works because FDE teams compound product development insights that improve the core platform.

I doubt teams can win large enterprise deals without FDE capability anymore.

Customers expect embedded engineering support, not just API access and documentation.

The companies that industrialize this model first will win the enterprise AI market.

How is your team handling custom implementations right now?
