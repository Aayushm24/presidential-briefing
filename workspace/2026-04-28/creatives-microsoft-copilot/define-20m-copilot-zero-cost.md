# Define — 20m-copilot-zero-cost

## Source post

20 million people pay Microsoft for Copilot every month.

And Microsoft now pays $0 for the models running it.

That's the deal Satya signed last week.

"We fully plan to exploit it" was his exact phrasing on the earnings call.

Two narratives died in that sentence.

The first: "nobody actually uses AI in enterprise."

20 million paid seats with daily engagement is not a pilot. Not a procurement experiment. Not even a budget line waiting to get cut.

That's habit-level adoption inside the tools people already open every morning.

The second: "we'll win on model quality."

If the largest distributor in software gets frontier models royalty-free through 2032, "better model" stops being a moat.

It becomes a feature Microsoft ships for free inside Word, Excel, Teams.

Everywhere on the internet, I still see people pitch:

- better latency
- better routing
- better reasoning

All of them are real but none defensible against a cost curve that runs to zero for the incumbent.

The middle of the stack is getting compressed from both sides.

Distribution from above. Subsidized intelligence from below.

The wrapper layer. The orchestration layer. The thin agent on top of GPT.

All of it.

What still looks defensible:

- proprietary data loops
- regulated workflow depth
- vertical context Microsoft can't ship through Office by default

If you're building agents right now, what's your one bet Microsoft can't absorb into Copilot in 18 months?

## Position statement

Make a builder pitching "better model" or "thin agent on top of GPT" feel that the floor just dropped under their roadmap — and want to rewrite their wedge around something Microsoft physically cannot ship through Office by 2032.

## 3 conviction bets (specific, falsifiable)

1. **The paradox IS the post.** Two numbers stacked vertically — `20M paid seats` over `$0 in model cost` — will out-stop any headline because the post's central claim is already a math problem the viewer wants to solve. Any explanatory copy weakens the punch. Show the asymmetry, let it hurt.
2. **Compression as a literal squeeze.** The "middle of the stack getting compressed from both sides" is one of the only metaphors in the post that earns a diagram. Two arrows pointing inward at a labeled middle band is the exact visual the post is doing in prose. Any option that builds this should beat copy-only treatments.
3. **The Satya quote works as ledger evidence.** "We fully plan to exploit it" is a CEO admission. Set in monospace, dated, with earnings-call attribution, it reads as a document not a graphic. Quote-as-receipt extends the proven artifact metaphor without copying the price-tag aesthetic from the parallel post.

## Kill list

1. Will NOT use the Microsoft logo, Copilot logo, or any brand mark — the artifact stands alone.
2. Will NOT use multiple accent colors. One brick-red on warm paper, full stop.
3. Will NOT visualize this as a stock chart or "growth curve" — the post is about a structural shift, not a trend line.
4. Will NOT include emoji or AI-stylized decoration (no neural swooshes, no robot iconography).
5. Will NOT center the composition on every option — top-left or off-axis anchoring forces a scan path.
6. Will NOT exceed 14 words of text on any canvas. The post does the talking.
7. Will NOT explain the metaphor in a subtitle. If a viewer needs the subtitle, the visual failed.
8. Will NOT use gradients, drop shadows, or rounded-corner cards.
9. Will NOT copy the price-tag silhouette from the OpenAI $50B post on the same day.
10. Will NOT use a stock receipt template — if a receipt motif is used, it must be specifically a quote-card / ledger entry, not a transaction receipt.

## Success criteria

- **3-second test:** Viewer registers (1) the asymmetry between a huge paid-user number and a zero-cost number, (2) Microsoft is the actor, (3) the date 2032 OR the Satya quote IS the anchor. They do NOT need to read the post to feel "the floor moved."
- **Thumbnail test:** At 400px wide, the headline number(s) and the single accent element are still readable. Annotations can blur — the silhouette and the contrast carry the point.
- **Logo-swap test:** If you put a McKinsey logo in the corner, it should feel WRONG. The off-grid placement, monospaced everything, and slightly-imperfect linework should signal "builder", not "consulting deck."

## Design constraints

- **Color palette:** ONE accent — brick-red `#a8301f` (continues the established system). Canvas warm paper `#f4efe6`. Ink near-black `#141414`. Soft ink `rgba(20,20,20,0.55)`.
- **Typography:** IBM Plex Mono only. Weights 400/500/600/700.
- **Mandatory elements per option:** at least ONE of — `20M`, `$0`, `2032`, the Satya quote, or the "compression from both sides" metaphor. Strongest options stack two of these.
- **Forbidden elements:** see kill list.
- **Dimensions:** 1080×1080. 96px outer padding. `box-sizing: border-box`.
