## Recommendation

**Ship pick: Option 1 — Paradox Stack** (`option-1-paradox-stack.png`).

The paradox is the post. Two numbers stacked vertically — `20M` over `$0` — separated by a single rule, with one brick-red anchor word at the bottom-right ("exploit it"), is the strongest scroll-stop on the set and the closest match to the post's central argument.

Why it wins:
1. **Math-as-poster.** No headline. No diagram. Just two numbers that together restate the post's first two sentences. The viewer's brain does the interpretive work — that's why it stops the thumb.
2. **Single mechanic, full commitment.** No competing focal points. Hierarchy is decisive, contrast is binary (ink vs paper, with one red accent), flow is a Z that's almost on rails.
3. **Different vessel from the parallel post.** The OpenAI $50B post on the same day shipped a price-tag aesthetic. This is pure typography. Visually distinct, same design system.
4. **Carries the substance.** Date stamp, Microsoft attribution, `royalty-free frontier models through 2032` annotation, and the Satya quote-stamp ("exploit it") all anchor the substance without crowding the hero.

**Strong runner-ups:**
- **Option 3 — Earnings-Call Quote.** If Aayush wants to front the Satya admission rather than the math, this is the alternate ship pick. Reads as transcript evidence.
- **Option 5 — Defensibility Diff.** If Aayush wants to drive comments, this option's closer-question on the canvas seeds the thread directly.

**Hold:** Option 4 (2032 Horizon Bar) — concept is sound, execution slightly competes with itself.
**Runner-up:** Option 2 (Compression Squeeze) — the diagram is the right metaphor, but pure typography (Option 1) hits harder.
