**Verdict: SHIP.** The artifact-as-argument bet pays off cleanly. Minor revise option: bump the "AZURE" label one size up so it's legible at thumbnail.

## Recommendation

**Ship: Option 2 — Price Tag.**

The $50B number is the post's headline fact. The price-tag artifact encodes "we paid for this" in a single physical-world object. Hand-drawn imperfection + Plex Mono + warm paper + one brick-red corner = strongest scroll-stop in the set. Passes every test cleanly. Highest thumbnail survivability.

**Runner-up: Option 4 — One Answer.**

Pure typography option. Renders the post's most quotable line ("Now it has one answer, period.") as the artifact itself. Strongest if Aayush wants a quotable visual instead of an artifact metaphor. Logo-swap is sharp.

**Hold: Option 1 — Wall Breach.**

Conceptually the most ambitious — literally renders the post's central image. Ships if Option 2 and Option 4 don't land. Minor type-size fix on "AZURE" label needed before ship.

**Revise: Option 3 — Procurement Contract, Option 5 — Cloud Diff.**

Both have specific layout fixes (more vertical fill, less dead space at bottom). Hold for a future post where the artifact's textual register is more central (Option 3) or audience is more dev-leaning (Option 5).
