# Scoring Rubric

5 dimensions, each 1-10. Be honest. Most items are a 4-6. Reserve 8+ for genuinely important developments.

## 1. Significance

Is this a genuine shift, or incremental noise?

- 10: New paradigm. Changes how people think about AI. (Example: "Attention Is All You Need" paper)
- 8-9: Major release or finding that shifts the landscape. (Example: GPT-4 launch, Llama going open-source)
- 6-7: Notable release or paper with real implications. (Example: new SOTA on a major benchmark)
- 4-5: Interesting but incremental. (Example: minor model version bump, small feature addition)
- 1-3: Noise. Press release, funding announcement with no technical substance, opinion without evidence.

## 2. Novelty

Has the brain seen this topic before? What's genuinely new?

- 10: Completely new concept or direction nobody has discussed
- 8-9: New angle on a known topic that changes understanding
- 6-7: New evidence for an existing trend
- 4-5: Update to something well-covered. Same story, new details.
- 1-3: Rehash. Same talking points we've seen multiple times.

When the brain has past pages on this topic, score lower on novelty but note what specifically is new.

## 3. Practitioner impact

Does this change what someone building AI systems would actually do?

- 10: Immediately changes architecture decisions. (Example: MCP adoption means stop building custom tool integrations)
- 8-9: Changes how you'd approach a project in the next month
- 6-7: Worth knowing when making future decisions
- 4-5: Interesting context but no immediate action
- 1-3: Pure research or speculation. No builder action.

## 4. Discussion heat

Is the community talking about this?

- 10: Top of HN + trending on X + Reddit threads + multiple blog responses
- 8-9: HN front page or trending on AI Twitter
- 6-7: Discussion in 1-2 communities
- 4-5: Covered by newsletters but no organic discussion
- 1-3: Only the source itself mentions it. No community pickup.

## 5. Connectability

How many existing brain topics does this link to?

- 10: Connects 3+ previously unrelated topics (potential BRIDGE)
- 8-9: Directly challenges or extends a past position (CONTRADICTS or EXTENDS)
- 6-7: Related to 1-2 existing brain pages
- 4-5: Tangentially related to past topics
- 1-3: Isolated. No connections to existing knowledge.

On day 1 (empty brain), score connectability based on how many OTHER items in today's intake this connects to, not past brain pages.
