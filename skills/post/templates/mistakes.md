# Template: Mistakes / Myth-busting

Use when we can identify a specific misconception in how people think about AI. Taps into loss aversion.

## Structure

```
[Hook: the misconception stated as fact, then challenged — under 100 chars]

[Why people believe this — be fair. The misconception exists 
 for a reason. Acknowledge the logic behind it.]

[The evidence against it — specific data points, named sources.
 Not "some experts disagree" but "Karpathy's data shows X, 
 and Copilot's financials confirm Y."]

[What to think instead — the corrected mental model. 
 Specific and actionable. Not "it's complicated" but 
 "the actual pattern is Z."]

[Closing — reframe that sticks. One sentence the reader 
 will remember.]
```

## Constraints

- Total: 900-1500 characters
- Success-oriented framing (talk to people who build, not people who struggle)
- The misconception must be genuinely common, not a strawman
- Tone: "here's what the data actually says" not "you're all wrong"
