# Post Templates — 6 types

Each post follows Hook → Proof → Implication → CTA. Templates vary the proof+implication structure by story type.

Length: 900–1,500 characters per post. Hook under 70 chars.

When writing 3 options, each must use a different template. Default weighting:
1. Personal-discovery (default, top performer)
2. Contrarian OR dot-connecting (depending on brain connections)
3. News-take OR specific-number (depending on lead significance)

---

## 1. personal-discovery

**When:** Aayush has direct experience. Default format.
**Hook pattern:** B (Identity/Confession) or E (Specific Number + Surprise).

Template:

```
[hook — personal vulnerability or specific confession]

[1-2 lines: what led to the discovery — context, not setup]

[the discovery itself — what actually happened]
- [specific detail]
- [specific detail]
- [specific detail]

[the reframe — what this means broadly]

[1-2 lines: implication for someone building right now]

[CTA — invitation or specific thing to try. Not "Learn more."]
```

Example opening:
```
i've been sabotaging my own AI pipelines for months.

every agent i built kept losing state between sessions. i blamed the model.

turns out it was me. i was passing everything inline instead of using a memory layer.
```

---

## 2. contrarian

**When:** brain finds CONTRADICTS, or lead story challenges consensus.
**Hook pattern:** A (Contrarian Truth) or F (Direct Challenge).

Template:

```
[hook — sharp contrarian claim]

[what everyone believes — 1 line]

[why that's wrong — 2-3 specific pieces of evidence]

[the actual pattern — what's really happening]

[who's doing it right — name company / team / number]

[CTA — question that forces the reader to pick a side, or invitation to share their take]
```

Example opening:
```
"think step by step" is making your AI worse.

new research tested chain-of-thought across 15 models. all of them got worse.
turns out forcing CoT makes models over-reason on simple tasks.
```

---

## 3. dot-connecting

**When:** brain finds BRIDGE between two past themes, or today's stories independently show same shift.
**Hook pattern:** G (Dot-Connecting).

Template:

```
[hook — two facts that don't fit]

[fact 1 — specific detail, number, named entity]
[fact 2 — specific detail, number, named entity]

[what looks like a coincidence isn't]

[the connection — the underlying shift both facts reveal]

[what this means for someone building now]

[CTA — question about where this goes next, or an invitation to name another example]
```

Example opening:
```
Cursor raised $900M. Copilot loses money on every user.

same product category.
same year.
opposite economics.
```

---

## 4. news-take

**When:** significance 9–10 on lead story. Aayush has an original angle.
**Hook pattern:** I (News Take) or E (Specific Number + Surprise).

Template:

```
[hook — news + angle]

[what shipped — one sentence, specific]

[what everyone is saying about it — 2 lines]

[what they're missing — the actual story]

[1-2 pieces of evidence from the article or Aayush's experience]

[implication for builders]

[CTA — specific thing to try, or question]
```

Avoid: "X is revolutionary." "This changes everything." Be specific about WHY this matters to someone shipping product.

---

## 5. pattern-reveal

**When:** Aayush has observed a pattern across multiple experiences.
**Hook pattern:** H (Pattern Reveal).

Template:

```
[hook — pattern count + observation]

[the pattern — one sharp sentence]

[3 specific examples — named, numbered, real]
- [example 1]
- [example 2]
- [example 3]

[the common failure / feature]

[what separates teams who get it right]

[CTA]
```

Never fabricate the count. If it's "across 10 founder conversations," it must actually be ~10.

---

## 6. evolution

**When:** Aayush's thinking has genuinely shifted. Or lead story extends a past thread.
**Hook pattern:** D (Time Bomb) or J (Evolution).

Template:

```
[hook — "i used to believe X" or time-based]

[what Aayush used to think — 1 line]

[what changed his mind — specific moment, tool, or data]

[what he thinks now — sharper, specific]

[why others are still stuck on the old view]

[CTA — permission-giving. "Take this as your sign to..."]
```

Never manufacture an evolution that didn't happen. If Aayush hasn't actually changed his mind, skip this template.

---

## Format checklist (all templates)

- [ ] Hook under 70 chars, fits before the "see more" fold
- [ ] One idea per line
- [ ] Dashes (`-`), never bullets (`•`)
- [ ] No em dashes. Commas/periods/ellipses only.
- [ ] Lowercase "i"
- [ ] Bold 3–5 words max
- [ ] 900–1,500 characters total
- [ ] At least 2 specific numbers OR named tools/companies
- [ ] CTA is not "Learn more" or a question the algorithm flags as engagement bait
- [ ] Max 1 emoji
- [ ] 0 em dashes (hard-regex enforced)
- [ ] Max 3 hashtags at end, topic-exact
- [ ] No kill-list violations (see `kill-list.md`)
- [ ] Passes 15-point voice audit (see `review.md`)
