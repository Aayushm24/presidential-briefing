# External post-writing frameworks — distilled (background reading)

**Source:** Kleo's "Content Playbook" newsletter, 5-3-1 issue dated 2026-04-26 (Paolo Trivellato spotlight).

This file is **background reading only** — most of the templates below are creator-economy / sales-funnel material that doesn't fit Aayush's voice (builder-to-builder, no lead magnets, no course-selling). It's saved so the pipeline can cite specific frameworks rather than rediscovering them, and so future blueprint changes have a paper trail.

The blueprint (`config/post-blueprint.md`) only adopted **3 things** from this material — see the "What was adopted into the blueprint" section at the bottom.

## The 5 templates

### Template 1 — Origin story (Age timeline)
**Anchor:** Timothy Armoo, "compressed bio" post.
**Shape:** `Age [X]: [event/result]` repeated 8-12 times. Wins, losses, turning points. End with takeaway + practical lesson + motivating close + image.

**Key rules from the source:**
- List as many noteworthy beats as possible
- Use specific names and results
- Start each beat with an action verb
- Pair each one with your age at the time
- End with overarching lesson

**Does this apply to Aayush?** No. He doesn't write personal-arc posts in this shape. The closest existing precedent in his corpus is the "I quit my job" post (TIER 1 Vulnerable Victor in `post-blueprint.md`), which is single-event vulnerability, not a multi-decade timeline.

### Template 2 — Surface-level vs basement-level problems (the Generic Content Trap)
**Anchor:** Nicolas Cole, "I can't stop thinking about a TikTok" post.
**Premise:** Generic posts deal with surface-level problems ("Nobody replies to cold emails anymore", "Founders are too busy to create content"). To strike a chord, dig 3-4 levels deeper to fears, faulty beliefs, hidden obstacles.

**Annotated structure of Nicolas's post:**
1. Opens curiosity loop — "I can't stop thinking about a [piece of content]"
2. Quoted insight
3. Validates the point ("This is so true")
4. Additional reframe (3 steps of relatable cycle)
5. Names the main obstacle
6. Diagnoses underlying problem
7. Defines meta-skill needed
8. Raises stakes
9. Philosophical CTA
10. Personal bridge / pivot
11. Social proof
12. Intros product (lead magnet)
13. Feature/benefit list
14. CTA (comment for resource)
15. Bold claim (core idea) — closing image text

**Does this apply to Aayush?** **The reframe-depth principle, yes.** The full template, no — the lead-magnet/CTA shape doesn't fit. Adopted: a "reframe depth" quality criterion in the blueprint (see below).

### Template 3 — Test an unusual offer without pitching
**Anchor:** Erin Balsa's "LinkedIn hype girl" post — "A founder I'm friendly with paid me $500 a month to be his LinkedIn hype girl."

**Devices:** Unexpected reveal → repetition to reinforce → funny self-aware aside → common problem → key obstacle → character callback → manage expectations → casual endorsement → fosters engagement.

**Does this apply to Aayush?** Not as a full template. The **funny self-aware aside** ("OK she didn't call it a 'hype girl'") matches one of his existing parenthetical-aside variants in `post-blueprint.md`. No new addition.

---

### Template 4 — Case study with urgency signals
**Anchor:** Chris Donelly, "Julia Taylor landed a $250K contract" post.
**Three urgency signals:**
1. Hard deadline (e.g., "10 days left")
2. Expiring bonuses ("early bird resources")
3. Demand signals ("spots filling up", "our reputation has preceded us")

**Devices:** Case study result → founder reflection → why customer succeeded (mistakes avoided + path taken) → verification nudge → testimonial → product outcomes → who it's for → urgency 1+2+3 → CTA.

**Does this apply to Aayush?** No. He doesn't run cohorts or sell courses with deadlines. Saved here for completeness.

---

### Template 5 — Offer stack (3-tier "snacks")
**Anchor:** Jacob Pegs's offer-stack post.
- Tier 1 — Micro snack ($9): diagnostics, reports, surveys. "They know WHAT to do."
- Tier 2 — Small snack ($20-250): masterclass / step-by-step guide. "They know HOW to do it."
- Tier 3 — Bigger snack ($1-5k): service. "They know they can get there faster."

Closes with screenshot proof of payments / customer wins.

**Does this apply to Aayush?** No — same reason. He doesn't run a service ladder.

## The 3 micro-playbooks

### 1. Matt Barker's "Before → But → After" 3-word hook fix
> "Last April, I had zero LinkedIn presence. **But** today, I hit 10,000 followers (and average 3 inbound leads a week)."

The three tiny words (Before / But / After) contrast the reader's problem state with their dream state. Implies the writer has a proven path between the two.

**Does this apply to Aayush?** **Worth experimenting.** Adopted as **Pattern F (experimental, 0/5 corpus matches)** in the blueprint. Same treatment as Pattern E — usable when it fits, not a default.

---

### 2. Content diet (Chris Williamson)
> "Your content diet should be spirulina for the soul, not fast food for your amygdala."

Inputs determine outputs. Be intentional about WHAT you consume, WHEN you engage, WHO you follow.

**Does this apply to Aayush?** Indirectly relevant — the daily-brief pipeline IS Aayush's content diet for AI news. Not a post-writing rule, no blueprint addition.

---

### 3. Trolls are tools (Courtney Johnson)
3 ways to invite hater engagement to boost reach:
- Share controversial opinions
- Pre-empt what'll upset trolls, then write hooks that invite criticism
- Play dumb ("What do you mean?") to make commenters elaborate

> "The worst thing you can be when building your personal brand is forgettable, ignored and people-pleasing." — Courtney Johnson

**Does this apply to Aayush?** No. His voice is observation-first, not bait-driven. The contrarian register he uses (Pattern A) is sharp but not designed to provoke trolls.

## Creator spotlight — Paolo Trivellato (5 highlights)

### 1. Lead magnet = bridge, not encyclopedia
Don't write 50-page PDFs. Give one step of your service, a free trial, or turn an SOP into a Claude skill.

**Adoption:** Not relevant — Aayush doesn't run lead magnets.

### 2. "Don't ask them to book a call. Assume the Yes."
After they receive the lead magnet, follow up via DM. No pitch-slap. Ask: "Hey, I'm curious — are you facing [specific challenge]?" Spend 3-5 messages digging into challenges. If good fit, share a case study, then send the booking link.

**Adoption:** Not relevant — sales playbook, not voice playbook.

### 3. Anti-content-pillars — write to **3 reader awareness levels**
Paolo: "I'm very against content pillars. Instead, I focus on speaking to people at 3 different awareness levels."
- **Level 1 — Unaware** of the problem you solve
- **Level 2 — Aware** of the problem but unsure of solutions
- **Level 3 — Actively looking** for solutions

Every post falls into one bucket.

**Adoption:** **YES.** Added to `post-blueprint.md` as a pre-flight question (which level does this option target?) and to `.claude/skills/write-posts/SKILL.md` as Step-2 reasoning. This is the most useful single insight in the issue — it forces the writer to be intentional about the reader's starting state instead of defaulting to "I have a take, here it is."

### 4. LinkedIn punishes AI content
"I've noticed LinkedIn is punishing AI content. So I recommend getting on 50 quick calls with prospects (Amazon gift cards if you have to). Aim to understand the actual words people use to describe their problems and desires."

**Adoption:** Reinforces Aayush's existing voice rules around using actual customer words. The pipeline already pulls from his real Atlan experience entries. No new rule, but tagged as supporting evidence for the existing "no AI tells" stance.

### 5. "A post with 40 comments books me 4 qualified calls."
Engagement matters less than the case-study stack. Be your own case study. Share it everywhere — posts, lead magnets, DMs, featured section, website.

**Adoption:** Not relevant — Aayush isn't booking calls off LinkedIn. His goal is signal/voice, not lead generation.
